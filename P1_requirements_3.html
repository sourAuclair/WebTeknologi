Administrative details

Client name: DankMeals Contact info: DankMeals@gmail.com Our client is a student organisation called “DankMeals” that wants to promote easy and quick meals for students. The client wants to offer a recipe web-page for student, that makes cooking fun, easy and motivating. The funders of DankMeals have experienced themselves how difficult it is to find time to make proper food in a busy student life. And that unfortunately results in excessive eating of half-fabricates such as frozen pizza, which is both expensive and boring.

Purpose and goals

The purpose of the webpage is to provide easy recipes for students, to encourage them to make their own meals. Another purpose is to encourage them to develop their cooking skills, through more advanced recipes. The goal is for students to be more independent and be able to make a variety of recipes both for every day, and the weekends. The easy recipes are located at “Hverdag” and the more complicated ones are at the “Kos” section. The easy recipes can be used to get started with cooking, and the more complicated can be used to further develop their skills.

Audience

The audience will mainly be students of all ages looking to expand on their cooking skills. This is a site made for students, by students. Most students have a hectic schedule, low income and tend to take the easy route when it comes to cooking. Therefore, we have gathered cheap and easy-to-make recipes for any occasion, whether it is the everyday dinner, pre-drinks or a chill weekend. We market towards students, who tend to be picky with technology. This will pose a challenge in web design as the site has to be bug-free, functional, easy to navigate as well as stylish in order to captivate the short attention span of a student. Ingredients will have to be accessible at the local supermarket, and sensible for a student to buy.

Content & Organization

  • Contact: Contact information for the owner of the site (our imaginary client). There will be a list of sponsors, if applicable.
  • About: A section which explains the motivation behind the site and what we (the client) wishes to accomplish with the site.
  • Recipes: There will be three categories of recipes. Each category will have 3-4 recipes listed, with detailed steps and a list of ingredients as well as pictures of the meal. There will be suggested drink pairings with each meal. The list of categories may increase at the clients request if time permits it. It will also be possible for users to suggest adding their own recipes to the lists through a specialized contact form. The goal of the recipe section is to motivate students to cook more by supplying recipes for meals that are easy to follow and quick to make.
    • Party: Will include recipes fitting for a party setting. Examples include drinks, snacks and finger food.
    • Every-day: Will include recipes for complete meals, which are easy and quick to make, specifically for lunches and dinners.
    • Relax: Will include recipes for a relaxing setting, such as movie nights and board game sessions.
  • Try-your-luck: It will be possible to generate dishes and drinks at random. The goal of the try-your-luck section is to motivate students to experiment more, primarily with cooking but also drink-mixing.
    • Dish: A generator randomly pair together ingredients which makes up a dish
    • Drinks: A generator randomly pairs together different alcoholic drinks.

Functional and Non-Functional Requirements

Functional requirements

  • The user needs to be able to send suggestions for recipes
  • The user can generate “random” dishes and drinks from predefined list
  • The user will be able to customize the list of ingredients on a recipe to accommodate different serving sizes.
Non-functional requirements:
  • The user can visualize the spinning wheel that “decides” the composition of ingredients when try-your-luck is picked.
  • User can easily maneuver through the different pages and find recipes with at most 3 clicks, regardless of where the user is on the site.
  • User will always find a suggested drink to combine with the meal within the same page.
