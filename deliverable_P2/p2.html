Administrative details

Our (imaginary) client is an organisation for students by students, that has a current project to promote cooking. The client wants a website as part of the project where students can find recipes that are low-budget, easy and quick to follow. The members of the project have themselves experienced the difficulty of cooking proper meals as a busy student, and hope that the project website will help with the decision making of cooking, as well as improve the quality of students' diets.

The client is a subgroup of the student organisation, and has taken the name "DankMeals" for the purpose of the project.

  • Contact: dankmeals@gmail.com

Purpose, goals and audience

The purpose of the website is to provide recipes for students that are easy to follow in order to encourage them to make their own meals. Another purpose is to encourage them to develop their cooking skills, through more advanced recipes. The goal is for students to be more independent and be able to make a variety of meals both for every day and the weekends. The easy recipes are located at “Hverdag” and the more complicated ones are at the “Kos” section. The easy recipes can be used to get started with cooking, and the more complicated can be used to further develop their skills.

Navigation Structure

The navigation for the site indicates how we want the pages to link together, as shown in the figure below. As described earlier, the site has to let the users navigate easily through the different pages, and find recipes with at most three clicks, regardless of where they are on the site. In the storyboard, the pink here represents the elements which are always accessible from the navigation bar at the top of the page. More detailed descriptions regarding design and content here are specified in the Contents section.

Since the purpose of the website is to provide recipes, the structure should reflect this, and the recipes and the try-your-luck-functions are thus intentionally placed first, steering visitors to what we want most accessible, with further content available through a drop-down menu. Given that, our website has a hierarchical structure, a structure characterized by visitors starting on a home page, where they click through from a general page to more specific sites. Precisely due to the linking to subtopic menus, most users find this way of organizing information easy to understand and intuitive to navigate in.

Even though the site-organization has a tree-like structure, does not mean that visitors have to back-track to jump from one section of the page to another. This is due to the navigation bar at the top which is always visible (and also through the search bar), allowing for easy movement between the different content areas. Further, this type of structure, which works best with well-organized material, ensures that we have to be intentional in choosing our content, providing a framework for always keeping in mind the purpose of our site; Serving students an easy access to learn to cook for themselves.

Content

What follows is a list of all the pages that will be implemented in the website. The content of each page will be explained.

Each page will have a banner along the top of the page where it will be possible to navigate to other pages.

  1. The homepage

    The homepage is the first page the users sees upon entering the website. This page will contain the name "DankMeals" as well as a "meal-of-the-day" box with an image of the meal and an internal link to the recipe for that meal.

    Since the goal of the website is to get students to do more cooking at home while maintaining a busy schedule, the homepage should not be too cluttered or contain too much content. We opted for a minimalistic and clean homepage to make it as accesible as possible.

  2. About us

    The page about us will quite simply be about us, the project and its goals.

    The backstory of the project as well as its long-term goal, the different people involved and donors will all be included on this page. While the page itself will maintain a simple and clean look, there will be a significant amount of content on this pages compared to the homepage.

  3. Contact

    There will be a form users can fill out with a message in order to contact us.

  4. Recipes

    The page for recipes will contain

    • links to the three different recipe "repositories",
    • a link to a specialized contact form where users will be able to send in suggestions for recipes.

    There will be explanations for what the subpages contain, i.e. what kinds of recipes the user can expect from the three recipe "repositories" and what the suggestions form is used for.

  5. Recipe sub-pages

    There are three recipe sub-pages, containing recipes for different settings. They are

    The recipe sub-pages will all have the same structure and design. They will contain a list of all the relevant recipes. Each item in the list will have the name of the meal, an image of the finished meal and a short description. Both the name of the meal and the image will link to the actual recipe.

  6. Suggestion form

    The suggestion form page will be structured quite similarly to the contact page, but will include the fields

    • Name of the meal.
    • Serving size.
    • Ingredient (1 ingredient per field. Users will be able to add more ingredient fields).
    • Instructions.

  7. Try your luck

    The "try-your-luck" page will be centered around one mechanic: generating random meals/drinks by pairing random ingredients together. The users will be able to select either meals or drinks as the subject of the mechanic. The idea of this page is to both encourage the users to experiment more on their own and to humor them with possibly strange meals or drinks.

    There will be a number of fields (read-only), each of which will contain an ingredient once the meal or drink has been randomly generated. This will be accomplished by simple javascript code and a predefined list of ingredients and a few rules for what cannot be combined.

Minimum Requirements

The minimum requirements is fulfilled by having four distinct JavaScript applications, one in the page:

  1. In the try-your-luck section, the dish page, an array storing all the possible ingredients, within 3 categories (protein, sauce, vegtable) will randomly get one ingredient from each category and present it to the user on the web-page (when clicking a button). The same for drinks page, but here with 2 categories (type of liquor and bevegage to mix it with). These will be split into two js files, but have almost the same functionality. Emil is in charge of this section.
  2. Also in the try-your-luck section, both pages with show a visualization of going though all the ingredients in the arrays storing meals (in random order), which slows down with time. Then it will finally show the ingredient, which uses the functionallity in 1. Johan is in charge of this section.
  3. When inside a recipe, a user can change the amount of people the recipe is for, by using a textfield form element. This will then calculate the amount of the ingredients needed for the adjusted portions. This will be done by looping though the elements and e.g. multiplying each ingredient by two if the portion size doubles. Gry and Ane is in charge of this section.
  4. When inside a recipe, the user will be presented with a drink (and a link to where the recipe for this drink is, unless it can be purchased ready to drink), which is randomly generated from an array with drinks that fit that type of meat (e.g. a type of cheap red wine with steak). Solfrid is in charge of this section.
  5. Recipe of the day; Located on the home-page, it iterates though all the meals stores in the array, and shows one for a given day. Can also extend to meals of the week. Should not repeat the same meal, until all has been looped though.
